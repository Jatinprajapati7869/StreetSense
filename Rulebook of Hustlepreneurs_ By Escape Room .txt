1. Introduction
HUSTLEPRENEURS: By Escape Room is a dynamic entrepreneurial challenge designed as a strategic extension of Escape Room, combining the intensity of problem-solving with the innovation of the startup ecosystem.
The event is structured to test participants not only on their business ideas but also on their ability to think critically, perform under pressure, and develop practical solutions in challenging situations.
The competition begins with an online submission round where teams present their startup idea through a structured pitch deck. Shortlisted teams then advance to "The Escape", a high-pressure startup case-study round inspired by the Escape Room concept, where participants must navigate real-world business crises and develop the most practical and impactful escape strategy within a limited timeframe.
The final stage, "The Pitch", brings together the top-performing teams to present their startup ventures before an expert judging panel through a professional startup pitch.
Teams will be evaluated on innovation, feasibility, entrepreneurial understanding, strategic thinking, presentation skills, execution readiness, and market potential.
The objective of the event is to create a platform where innovation, leadership, teamwork, creativity, and entrepreneurship intersect while providing participants with an opportunity to transform ideas into impactful ventures.

6. Competition Structure

Stage 1: Online Submission Round
1. Reg Start: 4th June | 12:00 AM 
2. Reg End: 9 June | 11:59 PM
3. Mode: Online
4. Format: Teams are required to submit their startup pitch deck through the registration form.
The pitch deck should clearly communicate:
1. Problem Statement
2. Vision & Mission
3. Proposed Solution
4. Market Opportunity
5. Target Audience
6. Competitive Analysis
7. Business Model
8. Revenue Strategy
9. Go-To-Market Strategy
10. Scalability Potential
11. Execution Roadmap
12. Team Information
Screening Process- Evaluation will be based on:
1. Innovation
2. Creativity
3. Problem-Solution Fit
4. Market Potential
5. Feasibility
6. Business Understanding
7. Scalability
8. Presentation Quality
Result: Top 24 Shortlisted teams will advance to Stage 2. They will be notified via mail by 7th June.

Stage 2: The Escape (Case Study Challenge)

1. Date & Time: 13th June | 2:30 PM
2. Venue: ICSR Hall III
3. Format: Inspired by the Escape Room concept, this round places teams into realistic startup and business crisis scenarios.
Participants must:
* Analyze the challenge
* Identify critical issues
* Develop a strategic response
* Create the most practical escape plan
* Present their solution before the judging panel
Possible scenarios may include:
* Startup failure situations
* Funding challenges
* Market competition
* Customer retention issues
* Product launch failures
* Business scaling challenges
* Operational bottlenecks
Evaluation Parameters:
* Problem Analysis
* Strategic Thinking
* Innovation
* Practicality
* Teamwork
* Feasibility
* Communication Skills
* Execution Strategy
Result: Top 19 highest-performing teams will advance to Stage 3, One team who will be the winner of Escape Room will directly become one of the Top 20 Finalists participating in Stage 3. 


Stage 3: The Pitch (Grand Finals)
1. Date & Time: 13th June | 4:00 PM
2. Venue: ICSR Hall III
3. Format: Finalist teams will present their startup idea before the judging panel through a professional startup pitch.
Teams must demonstrate:
* Vision
* Innovation
* Business Model
* Market Opportunity
* Scalability
* Execution Strategy
* Competitive Advantage
* Growth Potential
Q&A Session- Judges may question teams regarding:
* Feasibility
* Revenue Model
* Market Validation
* Competitive Landscape
* Expansion Plans
* Execution Readiness
Winning Criteria: The Top 5 Teams will be declared winners based on overall performance across the competition.

8. Judging & Evaluation Criteria
What Judges Look For
1.  Team Strength 
   1. Do the founders have the right skills? 
   2. Is there a clear division of responsibilities? 
   3. Can the team realistically execute the idea? 
   4. Does the team demonstrate passion, commitment, and leadership? 

   2. Idea Uniqueness & Technical Innovation 
   1. Is the problem worth solving? 
   2. How unique is the solution? 
   3. What technology or innovation is being used? 
   4. Is there a clear competitive advantage? 
   5. Can the solution be built and maintained effectively? 

      3. Market Opportunity & Growth Strategy 
      1. How large is the target market? 
      2. Is there a clear customer need? 
      3. What is the business and revenue model? 
      4. How will customers be acquired? 
      5. Can the startup scale regionally, nationally, or globally?
